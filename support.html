QuickBooks Online integration

Why does GCC connect to QuickBooks?
When you connect your QuickBooks Online account, the GCC portal can render your invoices and project receipts in-app, surface payment status, and push line items back to QBO when you accept a proposal. It's a one-way OAuth handshake — Intuit owns the credentials, we never see them.
How do I connect my QuickBooks account?
Sign in, open Settings → Integrations, and click Connect QuickBooks. Intuit handles the OAuth screen; you'll authorize the GCC app and bounce back to your portal. Reversible at any time.
What scopes does the integration request?
Read access to customers, invoices, payments, and items. Write access only to invoices and items, used when we push a new proposal-derived invoice to your QBO at acceptance.
Can I disconnect later?
Yes. Same path: Settings → Integrations → Disconnect. Your historical data in our portal stays — we just stop polling Intuit and stop pushing new entries until you reconnect.
Does GCC store my QuickBooks credentials?
No. We store an encrypted OAuth refresh token (issued by Intuit) and the realm ID — nothing else. Your QuickBooks login lives at Intuit and is never proxied through us.

Project & warranty

What's covered under the lifetime warranty?
Lifetime workmanship — anything we cabled, terminated, mounted, or programmed. Lifetime parts on GCC-installed components. Customer-owned gear is warrantied per its manufacturer terms. Transfers with the property if you sell.
Something on a recent install isn't working
Email or call. If it's a workmanship issue, we re-run the drop or re-terminate the connector at no charge. Bring the original closeout package PDF so we have the cable IDs handy — it speeds the visit.
Can you do an audit on a building you didn't install?
Yes — takeover audits are part of the service-contract menu. Document the existing wiring + network, identify gaps, propose remediation. No retrofit pressure if the existing system is fine.
