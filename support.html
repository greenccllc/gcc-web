QuickBooks integration FAQ

How do I connect QuickBooks Online?

Sign in to your portal as an administrator, then visit /quickbooks.html or go to Financials in the admin menu and click Connect QuickBooks. You will be redirected to Intuit to authorize, then back to your portal.

What data is synced?

Customer records, invoices, payments, and items. We use Intuit's com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting OAuth scope — read access only, no permission to initiate payments. See our Privacy Policy section 4 for full details.

How do I disconnect?

From the portal: Settings → Integrations → Disconnect QuickBooks. From QuickBooks: Apps → My Apps → GCC LLC → Disconnect. Either method revokes the OAuth token and stops webhook delivery.

How are tokens stored?

OAuth refresh tokens are encrypted at rest using ASP.NET Data Protection with machine-bound keys. We never see or store your QuickBooks password — you authenticate directly with Intuit during the OAuth flow.

How fast does sync update?

Initial connect pulls the latest 50 invoices. After that, Intuit's webhooks notify us in real time when invoices, payments, or customers change in QuickBooks. We typically reflect changes in your portal within seconds.

What if a webhook delivery fails?

Intuit retries failed deliveries automatically with exponential backoff for up to 6 hours. If you need to force a manual sync, an admin can click Sync invoices on the Financials page. If problems persist, email support and include the realm ID shown on the Financials page.

Why does the privacy policy mention machine-bound encryption keys?

Our application server uses Microsoft's ASP.NET Data Protection API with keys persisted to a protected directory. This means OAuth tokens are tied to the server they were issued on; they cannot be exfiltrated and used elsewhere even if the database is copied.
