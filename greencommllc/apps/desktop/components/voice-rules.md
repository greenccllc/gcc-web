# Component: Voice Rules

Not client-facing. Governs how the generator writes. Referenced by the system prompt and QA checklist.

---

## Universal rules (all proposals)

- **Calibri only.** No other typeface in any deliverable.
- **Bullet lists only on client-facing copy.** Never numbered lists, except for schedule milestones (which are natural sequences).
- **Title Case for headings.** Never ALL CAPS.
- **Left-aligned body copy, ragged right.** Never justified.
- **Bold for emphasis.** Never underline.
- **No photos.** No site shots, no crew shots, no project shots, no sketches. Logo and simple dividers only.
- **First reference: "GCC LLC" in full. Subsequent: "GCC" acceptable.** Never "Green Comm Cabling" except in the legal name block.
- **Standard footer on every page except cover:**
  `GCC LLC · Proprietary & Confidential · Licensed & Insured · KCMO & STL`

---

## Forbidden phrases — body copy (reject on sight)

"In conclusion" · "It's important to note" · "Leveraging our expertise" · "Best-in-class" · "World-class" · "Industry-leading" · "Turnkey" (unless literal) · "Robust solution" · "Cutting-edge" · "State-of-the-art" · "Synergy" · "Synergistic" · "Value-add" · "Going forward" · "At the end of the day" · "Move the needle" · "Circle back" · "Low-hanging fruit" · "Mission-critical" · "Stakeholders" · "Deep dive" · "Holistic" · "Ecosystem" (unless literal UniFi ecosystem) · "Leverage" · "Non-Union Div 27/28 Contractor"

## Forbidden phrases — versioning leakage

Never appears in a client-facing doc: "revised" · "updated" · "v2" · "regenerated" · "rebuilt" · "latest version" · "refresh" · "regen" · build timestamps · generator IDs.

## GC-facing voice (bids / subcontractor proposals)

Formal, technical, third-person organizational voice. Signed by Kaitlyn Lim Morris as President & CEO. Audience: GC estimators and PMs reviewing RFPs against multiple subs.

**Sounds like:**
- "GCC will furnish and install Cat6A plenum cabling to every data location shown on E-series drawings."
- "All copper terminations will be certified with a Fluke DSX-8000 and delivered as a LinkWare PDF report at closeout."
- "The following items are excluded: fire alarm, BDA/DAS, lighting controls, BMS/HVAC controls."

**Rules:**
- Short declarative sentences in scope narratives.
- Scope first, price second. Every claim ties to a deliverable or exclusion.
- Credential-driven — cite certifications, insurance, bonding, prior work. Never adjective-driven.
- No humor in bid body. No hedging ("we'll try," "we hope to," "we should be able to").

---

## Owner-facing voice (quotes / direct proposals)

First-person from GCC to the business owner. Sales-focused, helpful without pushy. Technical content explained via **universal analogies** — things anyone can picture. Kaitlyn signs.

**"You" usage:**
- Subject of benefit, scope, or ownership: ✅ "You keep the lifetime warranty — it transfers if you sell the property."
- Subject of problem or shortcoming: ❌ Never.

**Analogies (max 1/page, 3/full quote, never stacked, never explained):**
Cars · Houses · Plumbing · Kitchens · Gyms · Coffee · Roads · Parking · Groceries · Post office · Sports · Weather.

Pick the domain the owner's business touches. Restaurant → kitchen. Doctor's office → cars over medical. Never reach for an IT or trades analogy.

**Humor:** Permitted and encouraged for explaining complex topics. Self-deprecating about GCC or the contracting industry is fine. Client-targeted humor is forbidden.

---

## Residential voice (homeowner proposals)

Warmer than owner, plainer than GC. First-person, but "you" is used freely as long as it's a benefit. No jargon — "Cat6A plenum" becomes "a high-grade cable that handles everything from today's devices through the next generation of smart-home equipment." Kaitlyn signs.

**Sounds like:**
- "We'll install the cable in your walls, test every line, and walk through how everything works before we leave."
- "Your network will be future-proof — the cables we put in will handle any device you bring home for the next decade or more."

**Rules:**
- Dollar amounts stated in plain figures with commas (e.g., $4,250), never rounded away.
- Warranty language is the same as commercial: lifetime workmanship that transfers with the property. No softening.
- No technical acronyms without a one-line plain-English note.
