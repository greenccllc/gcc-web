# Scope — Home AV (Residential)

**Applies to:** Homeowner TV mounting, in-wall speakers, whole-home audio, coax TV distribution.

---

## Inclusions

- TV mounting — flat-panel wall mount, fixed or tilt or full-motion, up to 86" diagonal
- Hidden wire install — HDMI and power routed inside the wall, with a recessed outlet kit behind the TV and a matching kit at the outlet strip below
- RG6 coax TV drops from a central splitter to each TV location — distributes over-the-air antenna or cable-TV signal
- In-wall speakers — 6.5" or 8" round ceiling speakers, or rectangular wall speakers; speaker wire 16/2 direct-burial plenum per NEC 725
- Speaker wiring to an amplifier or receiver location at the structured-media panel or dedicated AV closet
- Cable concealment — fishing cables behind drywall or through crawlspaces / attics where reasonable; surface-mount raceway (paintable) where walls are not accessible

## Deliverables

- TV mount level-check and secure-torque verification
- Audio-zone layout diagram
- Speaker wire labeling at the amplifier end

## Exclusions

- Receiver / amplifier selection and purchase (homeowner-furnished, GCC installs)
- Premium mount hardware beyond what's specified
- Drywall patching or paint touch-up beyond a 3"×3" area around each cut
- Outdoor speakers requiring new landscape trenching

## Standards

- NEC Article 725 (low-voltage wiring)
- NEC Article 820 (coax)
- In-wall HDMI cables are CL2 or CL3 fire-rated, required for concealment inside wall cavities

## Typical household package (for density-based quoting)

| Room type | In-wall speaker pairs | TV drops |
|---|---:|---:|
| Living room | 1 pair | 1 |
| Primary bedroom | 1 pair | 1 |

| Basement / home theater | 5.1 or 7.1 surround | 1 |

## Plain-English substitutions

- "HDMI active extenders" → "a booster that keeps the picture crisp over long in-wall runs"
- "Speaker impedance" → "whether the speaker wire matches what your receiver can drive"
