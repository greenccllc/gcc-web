## 04 — What We're Installing

**Home Network Cabling.** We'll pull 32 new Cat6A plenum data cables from every workstation, imaging device, printer, and TV back to the server closet. Each cable gets a permanent-link certification test — that's the equivalent of a pressure test for plumbing, but for data cable — and you get the test report as a PDF.

**WiFi Coverage.** Four ceiling-mounted access points distributed across the practice. The goal is no dead spots at the chair, at the front desk, or in the break room. We'll use the Ubiquiti UniFi system, which is the same kit we install in commercial settings — it's reliable and your IT vendor can manage it.

**IP Camera Surveillance.** Six IP cameras — two exterior covering the front door and the parking lot, two covering the waiting room and front desk, one covering the business office door, one covering the back hallway. All cameras are PoE-powered off the new switch; no separate power runs.

**Rack &amp; Switch.** New 12U wall-mount rack in the server closet. 24-port Ubiquiti UniFi Pro Max 24 PoE+ switch. Patch panel, cable manager, labeled patch cords.

### 4.1 Standards we work to

Independent certification is how you know the work is done right. We use a Fluke DSX-8000 tester on every cable — it's the industry-standard tool — and deliver a LinkWare PDF report that any IT vendor can interpret. Every cable, every jack, every patch-panel port is labeled per TIA-606-C, so if anyone ever needs to trace a run, it takes sixty seconds, not an afternoon.

### 4.2 Things we don't do on this job

- Fire alarm, nurse-call, or life-safety systems — those are handled by a specialty contractor certified by the State Fire Marshal
- Your ISP setup (you call Spectrum or AT&amp;T to activate service; we time our work to match)
- Drywall patching beyond small access holes at outlet locations
- Anything on the dental-equipment side — if it's hard-plumbed or hard-wired to a compressor, that's your dental equipment vendor
