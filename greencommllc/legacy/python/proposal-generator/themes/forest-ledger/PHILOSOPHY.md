# Forest Ledger
### A visual philosophy for the GCC LLC proposal system — reorganized forest-green option.

---

## The movement

**Forest Ledger** is the forest-green voice rebuilt as a *working dashboard* rather than a ceremonial folder. Where the first green theme carries the air of a civic brochure — color fields, headline-first hierarchy, consulting-firm formality — Forest Ledger trades that for the density and rigor of a trader's daybook. The palette keeps the deep conifer greens of the first theme but deploys them entirely differently: as thin spines in the margins, as quiet chips beside data rows, as a single vertical rule that runs the height of the page. Nothing is filled. Color appears only where it earns the reader's attention.

## Surface and signal

The ground is **warm cream** — a paper warmth that softens the geometric grid so the eye rests on the data rather than the page. **Conifer green** appears as a recurring mark: a 4 pt indicator square, a 0.6 pt vertical rule, a small green label tag. **Ink slate** handles primary text. **Moss** and **pine** (secondary greens) differentiate categories without raising voice. The effect is of a well-kept analyst's ledger — a document whose calm comes from organization, not ornament.

## Grid as argument

Composition obeys a visible 12-column grid. Scope becomes a bill of materials with mono numerics; the cover becomes a quick-reference card with labeled facts grouped in four quadrants; every figure has a nearby label set in small caps. Typographic hierarchy is minimal — the work is done by spacing, column position, and the small, insistent green accent marks. The result reads as something meticulously crafted, the painstaking product of an operator who has clearly filed a thousand of these and tuned this one to the quarter-millimeter.

## The temperament

Forest Ledger is the voice of a practitioner who likes instruments — a calibrated multimeter, a sharpened pencil, a clean spreadsheet. It speaks softly, in well-leaded sans-serif prose, and lets the numbers carry the thesis. Where Forest Canopy says *we are a trusted contractor*, Forest Ledger says *here is the work, priced to the penny, ready to sign*. It is the proposal as working document — the thing you actually file, with master-level craftsmanship visible in every grid tick and every digit's alignment.
