# Bark & Indigo
### A visual philosophy for the GCC LLC proposal system — bark-blue option.

---

## The movement

**Bark & Indigo** draws from the bound folios of an Atlantic trading house — the oxblood-and-cloth-covered ledgers of a 19th-century marine insurer, the navy-and-walnut interior of a wood-paneled library. It is a palette of deep, slightly dusted **indigo**, warm **walnut bark**, and **oyster cream** paper. The voice is senior. The voice is quiet. The voice trusts that if the work is good, the document does not need to announce it.

## Material and composition

The page is composed like a printed folio: a deep indigo hairline border set inside a generous cream margin, a serif heading set tight and lowercase, a walnut rule separating heading from body. Numbers are set in a warm monospaced serif — IBM Plex Mono — which feels like a typewritten register rather than cold tabular data. Key totals are accompanied by a small indigo bracket, not a fill. The sense is of a deliberate, archival document, the product of a firm old enough to have a house style and disciplined enough to protect it.

## Typography and rhythm

A single-column body runs tall and narrow. Labels are set in small caps, walnut-colored, tracked slightly wider than default. Titles use Libre Baskerville at large sizes with tight leading — the feel of a cloth-bound book spine. The rhythm is unhurried. Margins breathe. Every figure, every row, every rule is placed with the painstaking attention of a compositor setting type by hand — the sort of craftsmanship that reveals itself only on the second or third reading.

## The temperament

Bark & Indigo is the proposal as *ledger of record* — the voice of a firm whose clients are institutional, whose decisions are made by people with long memories, and whose documents belong on a wood desk beside a stack of contracts. It reads senior without reading stuffy; it reads patient without reading slow. Master-level craftsmanship arrives quietly, the way a good suit arrives: you notice the cut before you notice the color.
