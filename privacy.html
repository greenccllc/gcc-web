1. Who we are

Green Communications Contracting LLC (“GCC LLC,” “we,” “us”) is a low-voltage and structured-cabling contractor based in the United States. We operate this website and the customer portal at greencommllc.com to manage estimates, proposals, projects, and invoicing for our clients.

2. Information we collect

We collect only the information needed to deliver our services:

  • Account information — name, business name, email, phone, billing address you provide when registering or requesting an estimate.
  • Project data — site addresses, drawings or PDFs you upload, and details you enter into our estimator tools.
  • Authentication data — password hashes (PBKDF2-SHA256, 100,000 iterations, per-account salt) and session tokens. We never store your raw password.
  • Activity logs — sign-in events, IP address, user agent, and the actions you take in your portal account.
  • QuickBooks Online data — only if you explicitly connect QuickBooks. See section 4 below.

3. How we use it

  • Create and deliver estimates, proposals, and invoices for your projects.
  • Authenticate you to your account and protect against unauthorized access.
  • Communicate with you about projects, scheduling, and billing.
  • Comply with legal obligations (tax, recordkeeping).

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your data to train artificial-intelligence models for general-purpose use.

4. QuickBooks Online integration data

If you connect QuickBooks Online to your GCC LLC portal, we use Intuit’s OAuth 2.0 authorization flow to receive a refresh token. We request only the com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting scope, which provides read access to accounting data.

What we read from QuickBooks

  • Customer records (name, address, billing contact)
  • Invoices (number, line items, totals, balances, due dates, status)
  • Payments and credits applied to invoices
  • Items and the service catalog
  • Company name and basic company information

What we never request

  • Your QuickBooks login credentials — OAuth means you log in to Intuit, not to us.
  • Payments scope — we do not have permission to initiate transactions.
  • Bank account or credit card numbers.

Storage and processing

OAuth refresh tokens are encrypted at rest using ASP.NET Data Protection with machine-bound keys. Synced QuickBooks data (invoice headers, customer records) is stored in our SQL Server database alongside your other portal data. Real-time updates are delivered to us by Intuit’s webhooks; we verify each webhook payload using HMAC-SHA256 against the verifier token Intuit issues us.

Disconnecting

You can disconnect at any time:

  • From your GCC LLC portal: Settings → Integrations → Disconnect QuickBooks.
  • From QuickBooks Online: Apps → My Apps → GCC LLC → Disconnect.

On disconnect we revoke the OAuth refresh token with Intuit and stop receiving webhook events. Previously synced data remains in your portal so historical records (invoice timeline, payments history) stay intact. If you would like all synced QuickBooks data deleted as well, email us (see section 10) and we will purge it within 30 days.

5. Sharing with third parties

We share information only with service providers who help us run the business:

  • Intuit — QuickBooks Online API and webhook delivery (only if you connect QB)
  • Cloudflare — DNS, edge content delivery, and TLS termination for our domain
  • Anthropic — AI assistance for proposal drafting (only when you opt in by saving an Anthropic API key in Settings; the key is your own, billed to your account)
  • Google (Gmail API) — outbound email delivery for proposals you send (only when you opt in)

We never sell, rent, or trade your information. We do not run advertising on this site.

6. Security

  • All traffic is served over HTTPS (TLS 1.3 with ECDSA-P384 cert from Let’s Encrypt).
  • Passwords are hashed with PBKDF2-SHA256, 100,000 iterations, per-account salt — never stored in plaintext.
  • Session tokens are SHA-256 hashed in the database and bound to HTTP-only, secure cookies.
  • OAuth refresh tokens (QuickBooks, Gmail) are encrypted at rest with ASP.NET Data Protection.
  • Webhook payloads are signature-verified via HMAC-SHA256 in constant time.
  • Database access is restricted to the application service account; no public network exposure.

7. Retention

  • Account profile and project data: retained while your account is active and for 7 years after closure to satisfy contractor recordkeeping requirements.
  • Activity logs: 12 months.
  • Synced QuickBooks data: until you disconnect (then on request as described in section 4).
  • Backup snapshots: 30 days, then automatically deleted.

8. Your rights

You can:

  • Access and download a copy of your data — email us.
  • Correct inaccurate data — via Profile in your portal, or email us.
  • Delete your account — email us; we will delete within 30 days, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Export your estimate history as PDF or JSON from the Sessions page.
  • Disconnect any third-party integration (QuickBooks, Gmail) at any time.

California residents have additional rights under the CCPA, including the right to know what personal information we have collected and the right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights. EU residents have rights under GDPR including data portability and the right to be forgotten. Email us to exercise any of these rights and we will respond within 30 days.

9. Cookies and tracking

We use a single HTTP-only session cookie (gcc_sess) to keep you signed in. We do not use third-party advertising cookies, retargeting pixels, or analytics that share data with third parties. Your browser’s Do Not Track signal is honored.
