# GCC LLC photo shotlist

Single half-day shoot covers Google Business Profile, Thumbtack, Taskrabbit,
website, and social profiles. Bring an iPhone (15+ recommended for low-light)
or a mirrorless body if available; natural light only — no flash.

> **Single most important rule:** every photo must be one you actually took.
> Stock images get rejected by Google, flagged by Thumbtack moderators, and
> trash trust on Taskrabbit. Generic = not yours.

---

## Tech specs

| Use case | Min dimensions | Aspect ratio | File format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Business cover | 1024 × 576 | 16:9 | JPG, ≤5 MB |
| Google Business profile photos | 720 × 720 | 1:1 or 4:3 | JPG/PNG, ≤5 MB |
| Thumbtack profile + portfolio | 1080 × 1080 | 1:1 | JPG, ≤8 MB |
| Taskrabbit profile + tasks | 1080 × 720 | 3:2 | JPG, ≤6 MB |
| Open Graph (already done) | 1200 × 630 | 1.91:1 | PNG/JPG |

iPhone defaults are fine. Shoot in landscape unless noted; vertical OK for
Instagram/IG Stories crops.

## Shotlist (priority numbered)

### Brand essentials (do these first — needed for every platform)

1. **Owner headshots — Kaitlyn alone** — neutral background, soft natural light, business-casual or polo with logo. Three angles: head-on, ¾ left, ¾ right.
2. **Owner headshots — Nathan alone** — same setup.
3. **Owner pair shot** — Kaitlyn + Nathan together, in front of the truck or at a job site. Optional with hardhats for site context.
4. **Truck with logo, residential** — full vehicle, shot from the front-quarter, in front of a generic-looking suburban home (not a recognizable client's house).
5. **Truck with logo, commercial** — same but at a commercial parking lot or loading dock. Clean shot.
6. **Logo close-up** — magnetic logo on the truck door, tight crop. Square version for profile photos.

### Workmanship — close-up craft (highest converting on Thumbtack)

7. **Patch panel install — close-up** — tight on labeled cables, color-coded jacks, neat Velcro management. Backlit if possible.
8. **Open-frame rack — full-frame** — finished closet shot, ladder rack overhead, cables routed, no clutter on the floor.
9. **Cable management — before/after pair** — the same closet (someone else's bad job, then yours). Cropped identically. Shoot both at the same time of day.
10. **Fluke DSX-8000 in action** — testing a cable, screen visible showing pass result. Hand in frame.
11. **Fiber fusion splice** — splicer machine in use, close-up, finished splice in protective sleeve.
12. **Hand-tools and Cat6 spool** — flat-lay craft shot. Crimper, fish tape, glow rods, label printer, Cat6 spool, RJ45 jacks. Wood or concrete background.

### Outdoor / installations in context

13. **Outdoor camera with weather-sealed conduit** — bullet or dome, mounted under a soffit, conduit run visible and clean.
14. **Indoor PTZ on ceiling** — finished mount, no exposed cabling.
15. **Doorbell camera install** — Eufy / Ring / Nest, finished. Ideally a homeowner's hand pressing it.
16. **Smart home control panel** — flush wall mount, dashboard active.
17. **TV wall mount — concealed cabling** — 65"+ TV mounted, no visible cable runs.
18. **Mesh AP mounted at ceiling** — UniFi or similar, with the patch cord visible going into the wall.

### Active job site (shows process — big for Thumbtack)

19. **Crew member on a lift** — pulling cable through ceiling tiles, hard hat on.
20. **Cable run in a ceiling void** — J-hooks, neat parallel runs.
21. **Termination in progress** — punch-down or jack termination, close-up, hands in frame.
22. **Opening / closing a wall access plate** — shows the "we don't damage drywall" promise.

### Trust signals

23. **Certification wall** — physically tape your BICSI cert, MO contractor license, $1M COI summary, TIA certs onto a wall. Shoot as one mosaic.
24. **Branded shirts on the team** — group shot of 2-4 crew members in branded polos / hardhats.
25. **Hardhat with company logo** — close-up.
26. **Closeout binder** — physical binder open to the as-built drawings + cert reports. "We document what we install" trust signal.

### Contextual project shots (with client permission only)

27. **Senior living facility install** — wide shot of finished work in context (no patient/resident faces).
28. **School install** — hallway with cameras visible, classroom with finished AV, IDF closet.
29. **Healthcare** — server room or telecom room.
30. **Government / municipal** — exterior camera coverage of a public building.

> If client permission isn't available on a given shot, blur or crop out
> identifying signage. Never publish a photo that identifies a client without
> a written release.

## After the shoot — processing + delivery

1. Cull to keep best 30-40 frames total. Delete anything blurry, badly composed, or with proprietary client info visible.
2. Light edit pass (iPhone Photos or Lightroom Mobile is fine):
   - Warmth +5 to +10 (counters fluorescent install lighting)
   - Highlights -10 (preserves bright cable jackets)
   - Shadows +15 (opens up closet shots)
   - Sharpening +20
   - **No filters, no presets.** Looks fake.
3. Export at 100% quality, sRGB, max 5 MB each.
4. Save to `C:\GCC LLC\MyAiDrive\4-Company\photos\` organized by category:
   ```
   photos/
     01-brand/
     02-workmanship/
     03-outdoor/
     04-active-job/
     05-trust/
     06-projects/
   ```
5. Upload to:
   - **Google Business Profile** (10-20 photos, mix of all categories)
   - **Thumbtack** (10 photos — heavy on workmanship + brand)
   - **Taskrabbit** (5-8 photos — focus on small-job examples + trust)
   - `assets/og/` in the gcc-site repo for any new Open Graph variants
   - `marketing/photos/` if you want a portable archive
