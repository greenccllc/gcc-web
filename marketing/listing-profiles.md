### FAQ
**Do you provide the equipment, or do I need to buy it first?**
Either works. We sell the same Hikvision, Ubiquiti, and Eufy gear we use on commercial jobs at near-cost (no markup games). If you've already bought equipment, we'll install it — but we'll flag anything we wouldn't trust before we cut a hole in your wall.

**How long does a typical install take?**
4-camera system: half a day. Whole-home Wi-Fi mesh + a few Ethernet drops: one day. Full security + smart home + network bundle: 2-3 days. Fixed schedule, not "between 8 and noon."

**Will you damage drywall?**
Almost never. We use fish tape, glow rods, and an inspection camera to route cable through existing walls. If we have to cut, it's a small clean access hole behind a faceplate — patched, primed, invisible after paint touch-up. We document everything before, during, and after.

**Are you actually insured?**
$1M general liability + workers' comp. We carry COIs in the truck and can email you one before we arrive. Your homeowners' insurance will care if a permit-required job is done unlicensed; we're licensed in MO + KS.

**Do you do commercial too, or just homes?**
Primarily commercial — schools, healthcare, senior living, government Division 27/28 work. We take residential because we like the variety and because referrals matter. If you have a small office build-out or a multi-tenant building, get a quote at greencommllc.com.

## Rollout sequence

1. **Photos first.** Neither profile gets traction without 6-10 strong photos. Spend a half-day on a real job with someone good with an iPhone.
2. **Google Business Profile *before* either of these.** Google reviews compound across platforms; it's the dominant local-search surface.
3. **Run a paid Thumbtack credit pack ($50-100) for the first 30 days** — seed reviews by accepting low-margin jobs. Once 10x 5-star reviews are in, drop the paid credits and run organic.
4. **Don't link Thumbtack and Taskrabbit to the same email/phone.** Google has been known to flag the cross-listing. Use `tt@greencommllc.com` and `tr@greencommllc.com` as forwarding aliases.

## Discoverability notes

- **Thumbtack:** keywords most likely to surface this profile are *"low voltage" / "structured cabling" / "security camera install"* — make sure they appear verbatim in the intro and at least 2 specialty bullets. The $99 service-call pricing band matches the pattern that gets users to click through (Pelley's uses the same model with 4.95★ over 22 reviews).
- **Taskrabbit:** rate-positioning matters most. $115-125/hr for smart-home / network categories is the sweet spot — high enough to filter out hagglers, low enough to not lose to a competing tasker on price alone. The "What I won't take" section is unusual but builds trust fast.
