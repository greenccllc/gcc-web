# Google Business Profile setup — GCC LLC

The single highest-impact local-SEO move. Free, ~30 min to claim + verify, then
ongoing review collection drives all the downstream platforms (Thumbtack,
Taskrabbit, Yelp, Angi all weight Google reviews implicitly).

> Do this BEFORE the Thumbtack / Taskrabbit listings. Reviews on Google compound
> across every other platform.

## 2. Verification

Google will mail a postcard to 603 Seib Dr with a verification code (5–14 days).
For Service Area Businesses, video verification is sometimes offered as an
alternative — accept it if shown, much faster.

While verification is pending, the profile won't appear in Maps or local pack,
but you can fill out everything else.

## 6. Q&A seeding

Within the first week, post these as "Owner posts a question" then answer them
yourself (totally legit per Google's guidelines, and seeds the FAQ widget that
appears in Maps results).

1. **Q:** Do you do residential security camera installs?
   **A:** Yes — we install Hikvision, Ubiquiti, Eufy, and Reolink camera systems for homes across KC and STL. Free in-home estimate.

2. **Q:** Are you licensed and insured?
   **A:** Yes — licensed for low-voltage in Missouri and Kansas, insured for $1M general liability plus workers' comp. We can email a Certificate of Insurance before any job.

3. **Q:** What's your typical turnaround for a 4–8 camera install?
   **A:** Half-day install for 4 cameras, full day for 8. We give you a fixed schedule, not "between 8 and noon."

4. **Q:** Do you do commercial work too, or just homes?
   **A:** Both. Our primary practice is commercial Division 27/28 — schools, healthcare, senior living, government. Residential is about 30% of our work.

5. **Q:** Will you damage drywall when running cable?
   **A:** Almost never. We use fish tape, glow rods, and an inspection camera to route cable through existing walls. If we have to cut, it's a small clean access hole behind a faceplate that we patch and prime.

## 7. First 30 days — review acquisition plan

Google Business reviews compound. The first 10 are the hardest; aim to land
them within the first 30 days post-verification.

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Email last 5 closed-out commercial clients asking for a Google review. Include the direct review link from your profile dashboard. |
| 2 | Email last 10 residential clients from 2025-2026. Same link. |
| 3 | Add the Google review request as the LAST step of every job's closeout email going forward. Make it easy: link, suggested 2-line review, no asks. |
| 4 | Audit how many landed. Goal: 10+ five-star reviews by day 30. If short, follow up with the friendliest clients personally. |

## 8. Posts (weekly cadence after launch)

Google Business Profile posts appear in your Maps card and last 7 days. Cadence
of 1/week keeps the profile "active" in Google's eyes (a real ranking factor).

Templates:
- **What's new:** "Wired up a 32-camera system at [redacted senior living facility] this week. Full Hikvision NVR, 6 IDFs of structured cabling, all certified."
- **Offer:** "$99 service-call estimate for any home security install (waived if you hire us). Book at greencommllc.com/contact."

- **Product:** "We carry Ubiquiti UniFi APs at near-cost for clients who want enterprise Wi-Fi without enterprise pricing."

## 9. Tracking + maintenance

Once live, monitor these monthly:
- **Profile views** (Insights tab) — should climb steadily after week 4
- **Search vs Maps split** — if Maps lags, you need more photos + more reviews
- **Direction requests** — proxy for "people actually planning to visit"
- **Phone calls from listing** — biggest direct conversion signal
- **Review velocity** — aim for ≥1/month indefinitely

Set a calendar reminder for the **last Friday of every month** to:
1. Check Insights
2. Respond to any new reviews (good and bad)
3. Post one new "what's new" with a recent project photo
4. Update hours if any holidays in the next month

## 10. Common rejections / pitfalls

- **Wrong category:** "Electrician" is too broad and pulls in the wrong crowd. "Low Voltage Electrician" + "Security System Supplier" is the right combo.
- **Service area too wide:** if you list "United States" Google will deprioritize. Stay regional.
- **Stock images:** Google detects and removes them. Use your own photos only.
- **Suspended profile:** usually means the address didn't verify (postcard didn't arrive) or the category is too aggressive. Appeal via the Business Profile dashboard if it happens.
- **Duplicate listings:** if there's an old auto-generated listing AND the one you just claimed, request deletion of the old one through the dashboard.
